# California SB 978 (20212022) — Department of Resources Recycling and Recovery: wildfire debris cleanup and removal: contracts.

Existing law, the California Disaster Assistance Act, is administered by the Director of Emergency Services. Existing law requires a state agency, upon request of the Director of Emergency Services and to the extent that funds are allocated therefor, to render services and perform duties within its area of responsibility when considered necessary to carry out the purposes of the act. Existing law requires the Director of Emergency Services to adopt regulations, as necessary, to govern the administration of a disaster assistance program that includes specific project eligibility requirements, a procedure for local governments to request the implementation of programs, and a method for evaluating these requests by the Office of Emergency Services. Regulations of the Office of Emergency Services make debris removal from publicly and privately owned lands and waters, undertaken in response to a state of emergency proclamation by the Governor, eligible for state financial assistance.
