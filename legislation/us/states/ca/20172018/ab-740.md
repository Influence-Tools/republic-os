# California AB 740 (20172018) — Oaths and affirmations.

Under existing law, a judge or justice, among others, may administer oaths or affirmations. Existing law also authorizes a former judge or justice of a court of record in this state who retired or resigned from office to administer oaths and affirmations for a specified period of time if the former judge or justice requests and receives a certification from the Commission on Judicial Performance, and if a formal disciplinary proceeding was not pending at the time of his or her retirement or resignation. Existing law requires that an application for a certification include a medical certification establishing that he or she does not have a medical condition that could impair the applicant's ability to administer oaths and affirmations, as specified.
