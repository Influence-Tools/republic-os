# California SB 274 (20172018) — Administration of taxes: notice of deficiency assessment.

Existing law requires an individual taxpayer to notify the Franchise Tax Board of any change in the amount of gross income or deductions, as reported on the individual's federal tax return, within 6 months of the final determination, as defined, of that change, unless the change does not increase the amount of California income tax due. Existing law authorizes the Franchise Tax Board, within 2 years of the date of a final determination, to allow a credit, make a refund, or mail to the taxpayer a notice of proposed overpayment resulting from the final federal determination, as specified.
