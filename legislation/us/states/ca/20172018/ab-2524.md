# California AB 2524 (20172018) — California Retail Food Code.

Existing law, the California Retail Food Code, establishes uniform health and sanitation standards for retail food facilities for regulation by the State Department of Public Health, and requires local enforcement agencies to enforce those provisions. Existing law defines "food facility" as an operation that stores, prepares, packages, serves, vends, or otherwise provides food for human consumption at the retail level, as specified. Existing law defines "limited food preparation" for the purposes of the code. A violation of the California Retail Food Code is generally a misdemeanor.
