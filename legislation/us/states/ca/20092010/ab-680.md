# California AB 680 (20092010) — Local government: fees and judgments.

(1) Existing law, in the case of a writ issued to enforce a judgment, requires a levying officer to, among other things, compute and collect the amount of additional interest accrued on the principal amount of the judgment remaining unsatisfied from the date of issuance of the writ until the date interest ceases to accrue. Existing law permits the levying officer to adjust the amount of daily interest to reflect partial satisfaction, if the amounts collected periodically do not fully satisfy the money judgment.
