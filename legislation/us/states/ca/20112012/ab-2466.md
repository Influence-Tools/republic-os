# California AB 2466 (20112012) — Human trafficking: seizure of assets.

Existing law makes it a felony, generally known as human trafficking, to deprive or violate the personal liberty of another with the intent to effect or maintain a felony violation of, among other crimes, pimping, pandering, and abducting a minor for the purpose of prostitution. Under existing law, the crime of human trafficking is punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000 or by imprisonment in a county jail for not more than one year, or by both that fine and imprisonment. Existing law requires the court to order a person who is convicted of a crime to pay a restitution fine, as prescribed, and restitution to the victim or victims for the full amount of economic loss, unless the court finds compelling and extraordinary reasons for not doing so and states them on the record. Additionally, under existing law, real property used to facilitate the commission of human trafficking may be determined to be a nuisance and remedies may be imposed against that property.
