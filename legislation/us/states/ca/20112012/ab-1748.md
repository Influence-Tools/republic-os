# California AB 1748 (20112012) — California Community Colleges: fair market value of leases.

Existing law establishes the California Community Colleges, under the administration of the Board of Governors of the California Community Colleges, as one of the segments of public postsecondary education in this state. Existing law establishes community college districts, administered by a governing board, throughout the state. Existing law authorizes the governing board of a community college district to let, in the name of the district, specified property not needed for academic activities, and prohibits the fair market value of that lease from exceeding $25,000 per year, as certified by the governing board.
