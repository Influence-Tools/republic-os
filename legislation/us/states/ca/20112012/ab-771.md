# California AB 771 (20112012) — Common interest developments: requests for documents: fees.

The Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act requires an owner of a separate interest in a common interest development to provide specified documents to a prospective purchaser of that interest. Existing law requires a homeowners' association to provide these documents to the owner of the separate interest within 10 days of the mailing or delivery of the request, and limits the amount of fees charged for the provision of the documents to the association's actual costs to procure, prepare, and reproduce the requested documents.
