# Virginia HB 1778 (2021) — Removal of clutter from property; definition, civil penalty.

Removal of clutter from property; civil penalty.Provides that a locality may by ordinance require the removal ofclutter from property, or may, whenever the governing body deemsit necessary, after reasonable notice, have such clutter removedby its own agents or employees, in which event the cost or expensesthereof shall be chargeable to and paid by the owners of such property and may be collected by the locality as taxes are collected. Thebill defines "clutter" as including mechanical equipment, householdfurniture, containers, and similar items that may be detrimentalto the well-being of a community when they are left in public viewfor an extended period or are allowed to accumulate. Violations ofthe bill are subject to the existing civil penalty applicable toviolations of provisions relating to the removal of trash, garbage,refuse, litter, and similar substances from property.
