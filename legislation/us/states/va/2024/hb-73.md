# Virginia HB 73 (2024) — Unlawful detainer; expungement of action, entering of an order without further petition or hearing.

Unlawful detainer; expungement; entering of an order without further petition or hearing. Provides that in unlawful detainer actions filed in the general district court, if the 30-day period following the dismissal of such an action has passed&nbsp;or if a voluntary nonsuit is taken and the six-month period following such nonsuit has passed, the court shall, without further petition or hearing, enter an order requiring the expungement of such action, provided that no order of possession has been entered. The bill provides that if a judgment is entered in favor of the defendant, such defendant may petition the court for an expungement pursuant to the petition process under current law. Additionally, the bill&nbsp;retains the petition process existing under current law&nbsp;for unlawful detainer actions commenced prior to July 1, 2024, for which the court still has records.
