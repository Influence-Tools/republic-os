# Oregon SB 1121 (2025R1) — Relating to the disclosure of private information; and declaring an emergency.

</b> </i>] Creates the crime of unlawful disclosure of private information. Punishes by a maximum of six months' imprisonment, $2,500 fine, or both. [<i>Creates an exception in a consumer data privacy law for</i>]<b> Extends the time period, to July 1, 2026, during which </b>specified noncommercial educational broadcast stations<b> may cure violations of a consumer data privacy law</b>. <b>Declares an emergency, effective on passage.</b>
