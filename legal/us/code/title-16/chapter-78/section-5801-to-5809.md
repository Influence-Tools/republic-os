# 16 U.S.C. § 5801 to 5809 - Repealed. Pub. L. 115–334, title II, § 2813, Dec. 20, 2018, 132 Stat. 4602

## Notes

Section 5801, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 352, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1010, defined terms in this chapter.

Section 5802, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 353, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1010, established National Natural Resources Conservation Foundation as a charitable and nonprofit corporation to promote innovative solutions to the problems associated with the conservation of natural resources on private lands.

Section 5803, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 354, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1011, directed administration of the Foundation by a Board of Trustees and set forth provisions relating to its composition and operation.

Section 5804, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 355, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1012, related to appointment and hiring of officers and employees by the Board.

Section 5805, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 356, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1013; Pub. L. 107–171, title II, § 2506, May 13, 2002, 116 Stat. 274, related to corporate powers and obligations of the Foundation.

Section 5806, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 357, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1014, authorized provision of administrative services and support by the Department of Agriculture.

Section 5807, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 358, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1014; Pub. L. 105–225, § 6(b), Aug. 12, 1998, 112 Stat. 1499, directed auditing of Foundation accounts by the Attorney General.

Section 5808, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 359, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1014, related to release of the United States from liability for any debt, default, act, or omission of the Foundation.

Section 5809, Pub. L. 104–127, title III, § 360, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1014, authorized appropriations.

Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries

Short TitlePub. L. 104–127, title III, § 351, Apr. 4, 1996, 110 Stat. 1010, which provided that subtitle F (§§ 351–360) of title III of Pub. L. 104–127 could be cited as the “National Natural Resources Conservation Foundation Act”, was repealed by Pub. L. 115–334, title II, § 2813, Dec. 20, 2018, 132 Stat. 4602.
